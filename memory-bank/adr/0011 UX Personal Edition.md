The user experience (UX) for the **Reveila Personal Edition** is designed to shift the AI from a "website you visit" to an **"always-on infrastructure"** that lives where the user already is. 

Drawing from the viral success of OpenClaw and the 2026 "Agentic Design" trends, the UX is split into three distinct layers:

### 1. The "Command Center" (Primary Android/Desktop App)
When a user opens the Reveila app, they shouldn't see a blank chat box. They should see an **Agent Status Dashboard**.

* **The "Heartbeat" Visual:** A subtle pulse indicating the local "Sovereign Core" is active and its memory is synchronized.
* **Goal-First Onboarding:** Instead of a tutorial, the app asks: *"What is our objective today?"* (e.g., "Monitor M&A news," "Manage my calendar," "Draft my code").
* **The Sandbox:** A "Safe-to-Try" area where users can see what the agent *would* do before granting it permission to execute (crucial for building trust in the Personal Edition).
* **Model Toggle:** A simple slider to switch between "Performance" (Local SLM) and "Power" (Cloud-based Frontier models) while keeping the **Sovereign Memory** constant.

---

### 2. The "Invisible UI" (Messaging App Integration)
Most users won't "open" the app daily; they will talk to Reveila through **WhatsApp, Telegram, or Signal**. This is how OpenClaw gained its massive following.

* **The Personal DM:** Reveila acts as a contact in the user's favorite chat app. 
* **Proactive Nudges:** Unlike ChatGPT, which waits for you, Reveila sends *you* a message: *"I found a 10-K filing for the company you're tracking. Should I summarize the risk factors for you?"*
* **The "Approval" Loop:** If the agent needs to take a "High-Risk" action (like sending an email or spending API credits), it sends a structured message with two buttons: **[Approve]** or **[Reject]**.
    * *Self-Correction:* To comply with 2026 messaging policies (like Meta's ban on "open-ended" bots), Reveila's WhatsApp integration is framed as a **"Personal Productivity Bridge,"** focusing on specific tasks like scheduling and file management.

---

### 3. The "Watchdog" Experience (Biometric Interception)
This is Reveila's unique competitive advantage. Even if the user interacts through WhatsApp, the **Sovereign Core** on the phone or PC provides the final security layer.

* **The Biometric Intercept:** If the agent (running on your PC) wants to send a wire transfer or access a sensitive M&A folder, a **push notification** hits the user's phone.
* **The Action:** The user sees a summary of the intent: *"Reveila wants to: Send $500 to [X]."*. 
* **The Verification:** The user simply touches their fingerprint sensor or uses FaceID. Only *then* is the local "Sovereign Memory" unlocked to provide the credentials for the task.
